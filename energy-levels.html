Energy Levels of Bohr Model

Exploring the quantized energy levels in Bohr's model of the atom and their significance in atomic structure.

Quantized Energy Levels

Bohr proposed that electrons occupy discrete energy levels around the nucleus. These levels are quantized, meaning electrons can only exist in specific orbits and not in between them.

Electron Transitions

When electrons absorb or emit energy, they jump between these quantized energy levels. This process results in the emission or absorption of light with specific wavelengths, explaining atomic spectra.

Radiation and Spectral Lines

Electrons emit radiation in the form of light when they drop from a higher energy level to a lower one. These transitions produce discrete spectral lines, which are unique to each element.
