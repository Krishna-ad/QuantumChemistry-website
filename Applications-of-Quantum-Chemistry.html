Applications of Quantum Chemistry

Quantum chemistry is instrumental in understanding and predicting a wide range of chemical phenomena. Here are some of its key applications:

1. Molecular Structure and Spectroscopy

Quantum chemistry helps predict molecular geometries, bond lengths, angles, and vibrational/rotational spectra with high accuracy.

2. Chemical Reactions

It models reaction pathways, identifies transition states, and calculates activation energies to understand reaction mechanisms.

3. Material Science

Quantum chemistry is used to study the properties of materials, including surfaces, interfaces, and nanostructures, leading to advancements in technology.

4. Biochemistry

Applications include exploring enzymatic reactions, drug design, and protein-ligand interactions, aiding in medical and pharmaceutical research.

5. Excited-State Phenomena

Techniques like Time-Dependent DFT (TD-DFT) are used to study photochemical processes, such as photosynthesis and fluorescence.
