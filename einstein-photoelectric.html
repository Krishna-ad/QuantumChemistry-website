Einstein's Explanation of the Photoelectric Effect

How Albert Einstein’s insights into the nature of light revolutionized physics and led to the understanding of the photoelectric effect.

The Quantum Nature of Light

Einstein proposed that light is made up of particles called photons. Each photon carries energy proportional to its frequency, described by the equation E = hf, where h is Planck’s constant and f is the frequency of light.

Photon-Electron Interaction

According to Einstein, when a photon hits a metal surface, its energy is transferred to an electron. If the photon’s energy is above a certain threshold, the electron is ejected from the metal's surface, demonstrating the particle nature of light.

Impact of the Explanation

Einstein’s explanation of the photoelectric effect earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921. It was a pivotal moment in the development of quantum theory, showing the particle-like behavior of light, alongside its wave-like properties.
