The Atomic Nucleus

The dense core of an atom, holding the secrets of matter and energy.

Structure of the Nucleus

The nucleus is composed of protons and neutrons, held together by the strong nuclear force, despite the repulsive electromagnetic force between protons.

Nuclear Stability

Nuclei are stable when the balance between protons and neutrons is optimal. This stability determines isotopes and the processes of radioactive decay.

Applications

The study of the nucleus drives advancements in nuclear energy, medical imaging (like PET scans), and understanding the origins of elements through nuclear fusion.
