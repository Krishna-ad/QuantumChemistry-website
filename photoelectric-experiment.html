Photoelectric Experiment

The groundbreaking experiment that led to the discovery of the particle nature of light.

Experiment Setup

The photoelectric effect experiment involves shining light on a metal surface and observing the emission of electrons. This phenomenon was first observed by Heinrich Hertz in the 1880s.

Einstein's Explanation

Albert Einstein's work on the photoelectric effect, for which he won the Nobel Prize in 1921, showed that light is composed of particles called photons. These photons carry energy proportional to their frequency.

Applications

The photoelectric effect is the basis for technologies such as solar panels, photocells, and photoelectric sensors used in modern devices like cameras and light meters.
