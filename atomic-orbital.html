Atomic Orbital

The regions in an atom where electrons are most likely to be found.

What is an Atomic Orbital?

Atomic orbitals are mathematical functions that describe the wave-like behavior of electrons in an atom, defining regions where electrons are most likely to be located.

Types of Orbitals

Orbitals come in different shapes and sizes—s, p, d, and f—each defining unique regions in space where electrons reside based on quantum numbers.

Applications

Atomic orbitals explain chemical bonding, molecular geometry, and properties like magnetism, laying the foundation for quantum chemistry and material science.
