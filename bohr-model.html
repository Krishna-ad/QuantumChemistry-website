Bohr Model of the Atom

Exploring the quantum structure of atoms through the Bohr model.

Structure of the Bohr Model

The Bohr model depicts the atom as a small, positively charged nucleus surrounded by electrons that orbit in specific, quantized paths.

Energy Levels

Electrons occupy fixed energy levels, and they can move between levels by absorbing or emitting specific amounts of energy (quanta).

Applications

The Bohr model helped explain atomic spectra and laid the foundation for quantum mechanics, influencing technologies like lasers and semiconductors.
