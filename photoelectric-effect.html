Photoelectric Effect

The phenomenon that revolutionized our understanding of light and quantum mechanics.

The Photoelectric Phenomenon

When light shines on a metal surface, it ejects electrons from the surface, demonstrating the particle nature of light.

Einstein's Explanation

Albert Einstein explained the photoelectric effect by proposing that light is made of particles called photons, each carrying energy proportional to its frequency.

Applications

The photoelectric effect is the foundation of modern technologies like solar cells, photoelectric sensors, and light-based detectors.
