Wave Nature of Radiations

Understanding light and other electromagnetic radiations as waves.

Electromagnetic Waves

Electromagnetic radiation consists of oscillating electric and magnetic fields that propagate through space at the speed of light.

Wave-Particle Duality

Light exhibits both wave-like and particle-like behavior, as demonstrated by phenomena such as diffraction and the photoelectric effect.

Properties of Waves

Key properties of waves include wavelength, frequency, amplitude, and speed, which determine the characteristics of different types of radiation.
