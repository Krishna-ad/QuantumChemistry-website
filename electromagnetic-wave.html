Electromagnetic Waves

Understanding the waves that carry energy through space, including light, radio waves, and X-rays.

The Electromagnetic Spectrum

Electromagnetic waves span a wide range of frequencies, from low-energy radio waves to high-energy gamma rays. This spectrum includes visible light, microwaves, and X-rays.

Properties of Electromagnetic Waves

Electromagnetic waves consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields, moving at the speed of light. They can travel through a vacuum and do not require a medium to propagate.

Applications

Electromagnetic waves are crucial in technologies such as telecommunications, medical imaging, radio broadcasting, and even in solar energy generation.
