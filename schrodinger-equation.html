Schrödinger Equation

The cornerstone of quantum mechanics, describing the behavior of particles at the quantum level.

Concept of Schrödinger Equation

The Schrödinger equation describes how the quantum state of a physical system changes over time, offering a probabilistic view of particle behavior.

Mathematical Formulation

The time-independent Schrödinger equation is given by:
ĤΨ = EΨ
Where Ĥ is the Hamiltonian operator, Ψ is the wave function, and E is the energy of the system.

Applications

The equation is used to predict atomic structures, molecular bonding, and phenomena like tunneling, which underpins technologies such as semiconductors and quantum computing.
