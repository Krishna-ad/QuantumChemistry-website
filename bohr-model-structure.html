Bohr Model Structure

Understanding the structure of atoms through Bohr’s model and its significance in atomic theory.

Bohr Model of the Atom

Bohr's model introduced the idea that electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed, quantized energy levels. These orbits are stable and allow electrons to absorb or emit specific amounts of energy.

Electron Orbits

In Bohr’s model, electrons move in circular orbits around the nucleus without radiating energy, a concept that differs from earlier classical models. These orbits are associated with discrete energy levels.

Quantum Jumps

When electrons absorb or emit energy, they "jump" between these orbits, leading to the emission or absorption of specific wavelengths of light. This concept helped explain the discrete lines in atomic spectra.
