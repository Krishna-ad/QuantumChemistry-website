Rutherford's Atomic Model

Discovering the structure of the atom through the famous gold foil experiment.

Gold Foil Experiment

Rutherford's experiment involved firing alpha particles at a thin sheet of gold foil, leading to groundbreaking discoveries about atomic structure.

Nuclear Model

The experiment revealed that atoms have a dense, positively charged nucleus at their center, surrounded by mostly empty space.

Alpha Particle Deflection

Most alpha particles passed through the foil, but a few were deflected at large angles, indicating the presence of a dense core.
