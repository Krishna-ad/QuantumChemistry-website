Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

Understanding the fundamental limits of measurement in quantum mechanics.

The Concept of Uncertainty

The uncertainty principle states that it is impossible to simultaneously measure the exact position and momentum of a particle with infinite precision.

Mathematical Formulation

The principle is expressed as:
Δx × Δp ≥ ℏ/2, where Δx is the uncertainty in position, Δp is the uncertainty in momentum, and ℏ is the reduced Planck's constant.

Applications

The uncertainty principle has profound implications in fields such as quantum computing, electron microscopy, and the study of fundamental particles.
