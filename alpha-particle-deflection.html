Alpha Particle Deflection

The phenomenon observed in the Gold Foil Experiment, leading to the discovery of the atomic nucleus.

What is Alpha Particle Deflection?

Alpha particle deflection occurs when positively charged alpha particles are scattered by a nucleus. The degree of deflection provides insight into the structure of the atom.

How It Works

When alpha particles are directed at a thin gold foil, most pass through, but some are deflected at large angles, indicating the presence of a dense, positively charged nucleus in the atom.

Impact on Atomic Theory

The unexpected deflections led Rutherford to propose the nuclear model of the atom, showing that atoms have a small, dense nucleus surrounded by electrons.
