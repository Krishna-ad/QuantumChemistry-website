Gold Foil Experiment

The groundbreaking experiment that unveiled the nuclear structure of the atom.

Overview

Conducted by Ernest Rutherford and his team in 1909, the Gold Foil Experiment involved the scattering of alpha particles on a thin sheet of gold to study atomic structure.

Key Observations

The experiment showed that most alpha particles passed through the foil, but some were deflected, indicating a dense, positively charged nucleus at the center of the atom.

Impact

The Gold Foil Experiment disproved the "plum pudding model" of the atom and led to the development of the nuclear model, fundamentally altering our understanding of atomic structure.
